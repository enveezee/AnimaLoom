
# Kismet Engine: Character Metrics Deep Dive

This document provides a comprehensive dictionary and explanation of the stats, attributes, and mechanics that define a character (or "Agent") in the Kismet Engine. Its purpose is to establish a shared vocabulary and a clear model for the "tangled web" of character simulation.

## Tier 1: The Foundation - Core Attributes

These are the raw, fundamental statistics that every Agent possesses. They are the bedrock upon which everything else is built. Think of them as an Agent's genetic potential. They are generally slow to change, if at all.

| :------------- | :--: | :--------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Strength**   |  üí™  | *Primary Stat*   | Raw physical power. Determines outcomes in physical confrontations, feats of strength (lifting, breaking), and can influence `Intimidation` attempts.         |
| **Agility**    |  üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏è  | *Primary Stat*   | Bodily coordination, speed, and reflexes. Governs evasion, stealth, manual dexterity (picking locks), and performance in fast-paced physical challenges.      |
| **Intellect**  |  üß†  | *Primary Stat*   | Logical reasoning, memory, and knowledge recall. Key for solving puzzles, deciphering information, learning new skills, and executing complex plans.          |
| **Charisma**   |  ‚ù§Ô∏è  | *Social Stat*    | Natural charm, persuasiveness, and force of personality. The primary driver for social interactions like negotiation, leadership, and making friends.        |
| **Resilience** |  üõ°Ô∏è  | *Defensive Stat* | Mental and emotional fortitude. An Agent's ability to withstand stress, fear, and psychological manipulation. Acts as the primary defense against social and mental "attacks." |

## Tier 2: The Psyche - Personality & Disposition

This layer defines the Agent's innate tendencies and psychological makeup. It doesn't determine *what* an Agent can do, but *what they are likely to do*. This is the core of the "tangled web."

### The OCEAN Model of Personality

A widely used psychological framework that provides a nuanced look at an Agent's default behaviors.

| Trait               | High Scorer Tendencies                               | Low Scorer Tendencies                                | Gameplay Impact                                                                                                                                                           |
| :------------------ | :--------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Openness**        | Curious, Imaginative, Artistic, Likes Novelty        | Conventional, Practical, Prefers Routine             | High scorers are more likely to use "Explore," "Create," or "Experiment" cards. They are more receptive to strange ideas but may be unfocused. Low scorers stick to what they know. |
| **Conscientiousness** | Organized, Disciplined, Detail-Oriented, Dutiful     | Spontaneous, Disorganized, Careless, Impulsive       | High scorers excel at long-term plans and resource management. They are reliable. Low scorers might use high-risk, high-reward cards and are less predictable.          |
| **Extraversion**    | Sociable, Energetic, Assertive, Seeks Stimulation    | Solitary, Reserved, Quiet, Conserves Social Energy   | High scorers have a larger `Social Battery` and are more likely to initiate social interactions. Low scorers are better at solo tasks and are more easily drained by socializing. |
| **Agreeableness**   | Compassionate, Cooperative, Trusting, Empathetic     | Detached, Antagonistic, Suspicious, Competitive      | High scorers default to using positive social cards ("Help," "Encourage"). Low scorers are more likely to use `Rivalrous` or `Antagonistic` cards and are harder to persuade. |
| **Neuroticism**     | Anxious, Insecure, Prone to Stress, Emotionally Volatile | Calm, Secure, Emotionally Stable, Resilient to Stress | High scorers are more susceptible to `Fear` and `Sadness` emotional states and have lower baseline `Sanity`. They may have powerful, emotion-fueled cards but are less stable. |
